The long-term goal (how we’re building)

Most manga and manhwa sites don’t offer public APIs, and many block server-side scraping. Instead of promising a universal web scraper:

  • Official and API-friendly sources can be searched directly (e.g. MangaDex).
  • Scan sites are tracked through the browser extension, where the reader already is.
  • Any unsupported URL can be saved manually.
  • User-submitted source links help the catalog grow over time.

Try it: I’m building Manga Watchlist, an open-source tracker for manga and manhwa. It is not a reader and it does not host manga. The goal is one dashboard for everything you read across different websites. The extension detects what chapter you are on, the site saves your progress, and you can always open the original source again. It’s early, but the long-term goal is a community-powered source index — a real map of where series can be tracked on the web.

What we avoid saying

“Search every manga site”, “100 sources supported”, “Automatically tracks every website”, “Universal scraper”, “Read manga here”, “Free manga reader”. Those over-promise.

What we do say

Track across the sites you already use · Extension-powered tracking · Paste any URL · Growing source catalog · Community-powered source index · MangaDex direct search · Manual fallback for unsupported sites.
